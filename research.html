Research

  1. Fully Automated Profile-based Calibration Strategy for Airborne and Terrestrial Mobile LiDAR Systems with Spinning Multi-beam Laser Units [Full Paper]

    Advisor: Prof. Ayman Habib
    • Proposed a fully automated targetless calibration strategy to estimate mounting parameters between LiDAR and GNSS/INS unit
    • Validated the proposed strategy by calibrating several airborne and terrestrial LiDAR systems and comparing the results to those from previously existing feature-based calibration strategy
    • Proved the ability of proposed strategy to reduce misalignment in LiDAR-based point clouds to less than 2 cm

  2. [Master's Thesis]: Interactive Environment for the Calibration and Visualization of Multi-sensor Mobile Mapping Systems

    Primary Advisor: Prof. Ayman Habib; Committee Members: Prof. Melba Crawford, Prof. Wolfgang Förstner
    Bias Impact Analysis [Full paper]
    • Analyzed the potential impact of bias in mounting parameters of a LiDAR unit on resultant point cloud
    • Established an optimal target primitive and drive-run configuration for most accurate calibration
    • Developed an outdoor multi-unit LiDAR system calibration procedure for a terrestrial mobile mapping platform
    Multi-camera Multi-LiDAR System Calibration [Paper 1][Paper 2]
    • Extended the calibration procedure to use conjugate points and geometric features to calibrate multiLiDAR multi-camera systems onboard airborne or terrestrial mobile platform
    • Experimentally evaluated the proposed technique by calibrating several mobile mapping platforms with varying number of LiDAR units and cameras
    Image-LiDAR Interactive Visualization Environment (I-LIVE) UI integrated with CloudCompare [Full paper]
    • Facilitates the visualization of point clouds, imagery data, and their registration quality
    • Aids the qualitative evaluation of calibration results for a generic mobile mapping system
    • Tool for qualitative quality control of GNSS/INS-derived trajectory and LiDAR-camera system calibration
    • Allows forward and backward projections to/from LiDAR and imagery data
    • Equipped with several tools to aid image and LiDAR-based transportation and agricultural applications

  1. Lane Width Estimation in Work Zones using LiDAR-based Mobile Mapping Systems [Paper 1] [Technical Report] [Paper 2]

    Advisors: Prof. Ayman Habib, Prof. Darcy Bullock
    • Proposed a methodology for lane width estimation using LiDAR point cloud from a mobile mapping system
    • Developed an algorithm to detect and report areas with ambiguous lane markings, narrow lanes, and/or wide lanes
    • Built a software for the visualization of reported areas in 3D point cloud as well as 2D RGB imagery

  2. Pothole Detection and Patching Quantity Estimation using LiDAR-based Mobile Mapping Systems

    Advisors: Prof. Ayman Habib, Prof. Darcy Bullock
    • Developed a fully automated algorithm to detect and classify potholes along transportation corridors based on their severity using LiDAR point cloud from a mobile mapping system
    • Clustered and traced the boundary of potholes using region-growing and minimum convex hull approach
    • Estimated pothole surface area and volume to determine the cost and type of pothole repair
    • Built a software for the visualization of reported pothole points in 3D point cloud as well as their boundaries in 2D RGB imagery

  1. Wheel-based and UAV-based LiDAR Data for Plant Height and Canopy Cover Estimation to aid High Throughput Phenotyping [Paper 1] [Paper 2] [Paper 3] [Paper 4]

    Advisor: Prof. Ayman Habib
    • Generated Crop Surface Models (CSMs) at 30th, 60th, and 90th percentile heights to evaluate the ability of system to penetrate the plant structure

    • Obtained the time-series plant height and canopy cover estimates for several genotypes in order to utilize the phenotypic traits for biomass prediction

  1. Rapid Lake Michigan Shoreline Changes in Indiana revealed by UAV LiDAR Surveys [Paper 1][Paper 2]

    Advisor: Prof. Ayman Habib
    • Surveyed the shore of Lake Michigan located in Indiana using UAV-mounted LiDAR unit to collect 3D point cloud on three different days
    • Assessed the quality and accuracy of obtained 3D point clouds by comparing those with the existing point clouds acquired by NOAA
    • Finally, analyzed the change in shoreline by comparing the derived 3D point clouds and estimating the volume of sand eroded along the shoreline between different days

  1. I-LIVE: Image-LiDAR Interactive Visualization Environment

    Advisor: Prof. Ayman Habib
    Major Contribution: Integration of georeferenced data obtained from 2D and 3D modalities of data from different sensors, such as RGB camera, hyperspectral sensors, and LiDAR units
    Quality Assessment of Calibration:
    • Facilitates backprojection of selected 3D points onto imagery and also, an intersection approach for multiple light rays to locate the 3D point corresponding to a point selected in multiple images
    • The quality of alignment of points in the 2D and 3D modalities serves as a measure for qualitative quality control for calibration and GNSS/INS-derived trajectory
    Transportation Applications:
    • Image-based Patch Area Calculation: Inbuilt ray-tracing algorithm for estimating the area of any polygonal patch of interest along highways, such as concrete road patchwork or traffic sign boards, using a single image
    • Lane Width Visualization: Backprojects derived lane marking centerline points onto 2D images along with a color-coded scale bar for visual inspection of lane width. Also, facilitates an automatic navigation through reported areas with ambiguous lane markings, narrow lanes, and/or wide lanes
    • Pothole Visualization: A click-and-go tool for automatic navigation through images depicting the reported potholes along any surveyed area in order of their severity (i.e., their depth and area)
    Agricultural Applications:
    • Plant Change Detection: Ability to visualize data from different sensors from different systems on different dates allows for a close-range monitoring of plant behaviour, such as plant height and canopy cover over its growth period
    • Plant Counting: An efficient tool for collecting plant count data required for training neural networks. Enables navigation from one sensor/date/platform to another for ensuring a quality check on collected training samples
